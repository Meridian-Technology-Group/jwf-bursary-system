# Applicant User Guide

### Applying for a John Whitgift Foundation bursary

Welcome. This guide is for parents and guardians who have been invited to apply for a bursary towards a child's place at **Trinity School** or **Whitgift School**. It walks you through everything — from opening your invitation email to submitting your application and reading your outcome — in plain language, step by step.

A bursary is financial help with school fees, awarded by the John Whitgift Foundation ("the Foundation") to families who need it. Applications are **invitation-only**: the Foundation invites you, and you cannot sign yourself up. If you are expecting to apply but haven't received an invitation, contact the bursary team using the details they gave you.

Please don't worry if any of this feels daunting. You can complete the form a little at a time, over several sessions, on a phone or a laptop — and the bursary team is there to help if you get stuck. Take it one section at a time.

## How to use this guide

This guide gives you the whole journey from start to finish. Read it through once to get the lay of the land, then come back to the section you need when you need it.

Alongside this guide there is a set of short, click-by-click **walkthroughs** — one per task. Each one shows exactly which button to press and what to expect on screen. Wherever a task has a detailed walkthrough, you'll see a link like this:

- [Accept your invitation and set up your account](walkthroughs/applicants/01-accept-invitation-and-set-up-account.md)

Click those links when you want the full step-by-step detail. The complete list is in the [Index of detailed walkthroughs](#index-of-detailed-walkthroughs) at the end.

A few conventions used throughout:

- **Buttons and labels** you'll see on screen are shown in **bold**.
- The first time a money or tax term appears, it's explained in plain words — and they're all gathered in the [Glossary](#glossary).
- Lines marked 📷 mark where a screenshot belongs. The team is adding these as the screens settle; the guide reads fully without them.

## Quick start: your application in eight steps

Here's the fastest path from invitation to submitted application. Each step links to the detailed walkthrough if you want more.

1. **Accept your invitation.** Open the email from the Foundation and click the link. Set a password (at least 12 characters) and you're in. → [Accept your invitation](walkthroughs/applicants/01-accept-invitation-and-set-up-account.md)
2. **Confirm your child's school and name.** A short card asks you to confirm the school (Trinity or Whitgift) and your child's full name to set up the form. → [Tour of the dashboard](walkthroughs/applicants/05-tour-of-the-dashboard.md)
3. **Complete the ten sections, saving as you go.** Work through the form in any order. Each section saves on its own, so you can stop and come back. → [Completing your application](#completing-your-application)
4. **Upload your documents.** Each document has its own slot — birth certificate, passports, P60s, bank statements, council tax bill, and so on. → [Upload a document](walkthroughs/applicants/16-upload-a-document.md)
5. **Check the review page.** When you reach the end you'll see a review screen that lists anything still missing, with a "Go fix this" link to each. → [The pre-submission review page](walkthroughs/applicants/21-pre-submission-review-page.md)
6. **Read and sign the declaration.** Tick the box, type your name as your signature. → [Section 10 — Declaration](walkthroughs/applicants/15-section-10-declaration.md)
7. **Submit.** Click **Submit Application**. You'll see a confirmation page with your application reference — write it down. → [Submit your application](walkthroughs/applicants/22-submit-application.md)
8. **Watch for the confirmation email.** Within a few minutes you should get an email confirming your application was received. After that, you can sign in any time to [check your status](walkthroughs/applicants/23-check-application-status.md).

That's the whole journey. The rest of this guide fills in the detail.

## Before you begin: what you'll need

Most of the time spent on a bursary application goes on gathering documents. If you collect everything first, the form itself is quick. Here's the full list — gather whatever applies to your family.

### Documents to gather

- **Your child's full birth certificate** — it must show the child's name, date of birth, place of birth, and the parents' names.
- **Passports** for everyone in the household (both parents, the child, any other dependent children, and any dependent elderly relatives). For anyone who is not a British citizen, you'll also need their **right-to-remain document** — a visa or **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)** paper.
- **Your most recent P60** for each parent — the end-of-year summary your employer gives you showing your total pay and tax.
- If a parent is self-employed or files a tax return: your latest **HMRC Self Assessment** return (sometimes called an **SA302**) and, for company directors, your **latest certified/audited accounts** and **balance sheet**.
- **Three months of bank statements** for each parent's main account (and any other accounts). Three consecutive months from the last six is fine.
- Your **current council tax bill** (or other evidence for the home you live in).
- **Benefit award letters**, if anyone receives benefits or tax credits.
- Evidence for anything special: a **court order** for school fees, an **insurance/education plan** for fees, a **decree absolute** (divorce), a **lease or rent statement**, a **redundancy letter**, a **sick note**, or a **care-home invoice** — only the items that apply to you.

You don't need everything before you start — you can save your progress and add documents later. But having them ready makes the whole thing far smoother.

### Accepted file types and sizes

- Files can be **PDF**, **JPEG (.jpg/.jpeg)**, or **PNG**.
- Each file can be up to **20 MB**.
- A photo taken on your phone is perfectly acceptable for a paper document.

See [What file types and sizes are accepted](walkthroughs/applicants/19-file-types-and-sizes.md) for tips on shrinking a file that's too big or converting an unsupported format.

### You can do this on a phone, over several sessions

The portal works on any modern phone (iPhone or Android) as well as on a laptop. Your work saves section by section, so you can start on your phone — handy for photographing paper documents on the spot — and finish later on a laptop. Your progress follows you between devices.

## Getting in

Everything starts with the invitation email from the Foundation.

### Accepting your invitation and setting a password

The subject line of the invitation will mention "bursary" or "invitation". Open it and click the link inside. You'll land on a page titled **Create your account**.

- Your **email address** is shown at the top and can't be changed — it has to match the address the Foundation invited.
- Check that your **first name** and **last name** are correct; you can fix a spelling here.
- Choose a **password** of at least **12 characters**. Make it unique — the system checks it against a list of known-leaked passwords and will reject anything too common. A short phrase of three random words works well.
- Type the password again to confirm, then click **Create account**.

You'll be signed in straight away. The first thing you'll see is a short card, **Let's set up your application**, asking you to confirm the **school** your child has been offered a place at and your **child's full name** as it appears on the birth certificate. Confirm both, and you're into the form.

> 📷 *Screenshot: the "Create your account" page showing the read-only email, name fields, and password boxes.*

Full detail: [Accept your invitation and set up your account](walkthroughs/applicants/01-accept-invitation-and-set-up-account.md).

### Logging in after the first time

Once your account exists, you can sign in any time at the portal login page. You'll see **Sign in to your account** with an **Email address** field, a **Password** field, and a **Sign in** button. Use the same email and password you set up.

### If your invitation link has expired

Invitation links are time-limited and can only be used once. If yours has expired or been used already, you'll see a page reading **Invitation invalid** with a short explanation and a **Back to sign in** button. Nothing is lost. Email the bursary team (the address is in your original invitation), tell them the child's name and roughly when the invitation was sent, and they'll send a fresh link.

If the link was already used, you may simply have an account already — try signing in, or [reset your password](walkthroughs/applicants/03-reset-forgotten-password.md). Full detail: [If your invitation link expired or doesn't work](walkthroughs/applicants/02-invitation-link-expired.md).

### Resetting a forgotten password

Forgotten your password? On the login page, click **Forgot password?** below the password box. On the **Reset your password** page, type your email and click **Send reset link**. You'll see a green **Check your email** message, and a reset link will arrive by email. Click it and choose a new password (again, at least 12 characters).

For your privacy, the system never tells you whether an email address is registered — so the "check your email" message appears whether or not an account exists. If no email arrives, wait a couple of minutes and check your spam folder. Full detail: [Reset a forgotten password](walkthroughs/applicants/03-reset-forgotten-password.md).

### Logging in on your phone

Open the portal address (the same link from your invitation email) in your phone's browser — Safari or Chrome on iPhone, Chrome on Android. Sign in as normal. A few things look different on a small screen:

- The list of form sections, which sits in a sidebar on a laptop, hides behind a **menu icon** at the top. Tap it to see the sections.
- A **bottom bar** stays in view as you scroll, with **Back** on the left and **Save and Continue** on the right.
- Upload buttons let you **Take a photo** or **Choose from library**.

Tip: add the portal to your home screen for one-tap access (Safari: share icon → **Add to Home Screen**; Chrome: three-dot menu → **Add to Home screen**). Full detail: [Log in on your phone](walkthroughs/applicants/04-log-in-on-your-phone.md).

## Finding your way around

When you sign in, you land on your **dashboard** — your home base. It tells you, at a glance, where you are with your application.

> 📷 *Screenshot: the dashboard showing the "Welcome back" greeting, the application status card with a progress bar, and the "Continue Application" and "View Status" cards.*

At the top you'll see a friendly **Welcome back** greeting and a line naming your assessment round (for example, *2026/27 Assessment Round*).

**Application status card.** A white card shows:

- An **Application status** label.
- The round name.
- A coloured **status badge** — a brand new application shows **Draft**.
- A progress bar reading **Sections complete — X of 10**, with a gold bar showing how far along you are.

**Quick actions.** Two cards beneath:

- **Continue Application** — picks up wherever you left off. This is the button you'll use most.
- **View Status** — opens the detailed status page with the full timeline (more useful after you've submitted).

**The section list.** On a laptop, the left sidebar lists the ten sections of the form (behind the menu icon on a phone). Each section has an indicator so you know where you stand:

- A **grey circle** — not started.
- A **gold bar with a fraction** — partly filled.
- A **green tick** — complete.
- An **amber warning** — saved but something still needs fixing.

You can click any section to jump straight to it. You don't have to fill the form in order.

If you've accepted your invitation but not yet started, you'll instead see the **Let's set up your application** card asking you to confirm the school and child's name. Once you've done that one-time step, the dashboard switches to the progress view above. Full detail: [Tour of the dashboard](walkthroughs/applicants/05-tour-of-the-dashboard.md).

## Completing your application

The form has **ten sections**. You can complete them in any order and save as you go — your work is kept safely on the Foundation's servers between sessions. Each section ends with a **Save and Continue** button; pressing it checks your answers and, if anything required is missing, shows an error summary at the top with the problem fields highlighted in red.

Two pieces of logic shape what you see:

- **Sole parent?** If you tick that you're applying as a sole parent in Section 3, the second parent's blocks disappear throughout the form (and you'll only see one income column in Section 7, one bank-statement slot in Section 8, and so on).
- **Re-assessment?** If this is a return visit in a later year, **Section 2 (Family Identification) is skipped entirely** and several sections come pre-filled. See [Returning families](#returning-families-re-assessment-year-two-and-beyond).

Here's what each section asks for.

### Section 1 — Details of Child

Confirms who the application is for. You'll choose the **school** (Trinity or Whitgift), the **school year** the child is applying to enter (Year 6, 7, 9, 12, or Other — contact the team if Other), and the child's **full name, gender, date of birth, and place of birth**. You'll **upload the birth certificate** here, and confirm whether the child lives at the same address as Parent/Guardian 1 (if not, you add their address). Finally, the **school the child currently attends** and the **start date** there. Full detail: [Section 1 — Details of Child](walkthroughs/applicants/06-section-1-details-of-child.md).

### Section 2 — Family Identification

Identity documents for **every member of the household** — the child, both parents (or the sole parent), any other dependent children, and any dependent elderly relatives. Click **Add family member**, type the person's name, then upload their **passport** (and, for anyone who is not a British citizen, their **right-to-remain / ILR** document). Repeat for each person. **This section does not appear for re-assessments** — you only complete it in year one. Full detail: [Section 2 — Family Identification](walkthroughs/applicants/07-section-2-family-identification.md).

### Section 3 — Parent / Guardian Details

Contact details, relationship status, and employment for each parent or guardian. At the top, answer **Are you a sole parent?** — answer **Yes** and the Parent 2 block disappears; answer **No** and both parents' blocks show. For each parent you give a title, name, date of birth, nationality, home address, contact details, and **employment status** (employed, self-employed, unemployed, retired, and so on). The status you pick here decides which income rows and document slots appear later in Section 7, so choose carefully. Full detail: [Section 3 — Parent / Guardian Details](walkthroughs/applicants/08-section-3-parent-guardian-details.md).

### Section 4 — Dependent Children

A table listing every dependent child in your household — the child this application is for **and** any siblings. Click **Add dependent child**, then for each one give the name, the school they attend, whether they are the child named on this application (tick this on exactly one row), any unearned income (such as interest on accounts in their name, entered as a yearly figure or 0), and whether they receive a bursary or have fees paid by someone else. Full detail: [Section 4 — Dependent Children](walkthroughs/applicants/09-section-4-dependent-children.md).

### Section 5 — Dependent Elderly

Any elderly relatives you support, both at home and in a care home. If you support none, this takes about ten seconds — just answer **No** twice. If you do, you'll add each relative's name, your relationship to them, and the approximate annual cost (estimate to the nearest £100 — the team wants the scale, not exact figures). For relatives in care you'll also give the care-home name and your annual contribution. Full detail: [Section 5 — Dependent Elderly](walkthroughs/applicants/10-section-5-dependent-elderly.md).

### Section 6 — Other Information

Three optional topics: a **court order** for paying school fees, **insurance policies** taken out specifically to pay fees, and **outstanding school fees** from previous years at any school. If none apply, this is three **No** answers. Where you answer **Yes**, the relevant amount fields appear and you'll be asked to upload evidence (such as the court order or policy schedule). Full detail: [Section 6 — Other Information](walkthroughs/applicants/11-section-6-other-information.md).

### Section 7 — Parents' Income

Each parent's **gross income** (your income before tax is taken off) from every source, for the financial year ended 5 April. You'll see one column per parent — or just one if you're a sole parent. Work down each row (salary and pensions, bonuses, benefits, tax credits, interest, dividends, rent, bonds, maintenance, bursaries, and other income), entering a figure or **0** in every row. A running **Total** appears underneath. Use **annual** figures — multiply monthly amounts by 12.

You'll also upload supporting documents here. Your **most recent P60** is always required for each parent. Other documents are asked for depending on what you enter — for example a **Self Assessment (SA302)** if you reported dividends, rent, or bond income, or a **benefits award letter** if you reported benefits or tax credits. The slot label tells you exactly what goes where (for example, "P60 — Parent 1"). Full detail: [Section 7 — Parents' Income](walkthroughs/applicants/12-section-7-parents-income.md).

### Section 8 — Assets & Liabilities

What the family owns and owes: your main home (owned, mortgaged, or rented, plus its value and any mortgage), any other properties, vehicles, savings, ISAs and shares, premium bonds, and any loans or hire-purchase agreements. Use rough valuations — online estimates for property are fine, and round savings to the nearest £100.

This section also holds two important uploads: your **council tax bill** (always required) and **three months of bank statements** for each parent's main account, added as separate files into one slot. Full detail: [Section 8 — Assets & Liabilities](walkthroughs/applicants/13-section-8-assets-liabilities.md).

### Section 9 — Additional Information

Your chance to flag special circumstances and add anything that didn't fit elsewhere. A short checklist covers six common situations — **divorced, separated, sick/unable to work, paying rent, made redundant, receiving benefits** — and ticking **Yes** on any row reveals an upload for the supporting document. Below that is a free-text box (up to about 3,000 characters) for anything else the Foundation should know: a one-off event, a recent change that won't show in the historical figures yet, or an explanation of an unusual number elsewhere. Keep it factual and brief. It's fine to leave the box blank if your situation is straightforward. Full detail: [Section 9 — Additional Information](walkthroughs/applicants/14-section-9-additional-information.md).

### Section 10 — Declaration

The final step — a legal declaration you read and agree to. You reach it only after the review page (next) has cleared every issue. You'll tick a box confirming the information is true, and type your full name as a typed **signature** (it must match a parent/guardian name from Section 3). Only one parent needs to sign, but by signing you confirm you're authorised to do so for the household. Full detail: [Section 10 — Declaration](walkthroughs/applicants/15-section-10-declaration.md).

## Uploading documents

Documents are a big part of a bursary application, so it's worth understanding how uploads work once — the same pattern applies everywhere.

### How an upload slot works

Every required document has its own **slot** with a clear label (such as "Birth Certificate", "P60 — Parent 1", or "Council Tax Bill") sitting right next to the question that needs it. An empty slot looks like a **dashed box** with a cloud icon, a short hint, and a **Choose file** button. You can also drag and drop a file straight onto the box.

To upload: click **Choose file**, pick your file, and watch the short progress bar. When it finishes, the slot turns **green** with a tick and shows the filename, the file size, a **View** link (opens the file so you can check it's the right one), and a **Remove** option. Full detail: [Upload a document](walkthroughs/applicants/16-upload-a-document.md).

> 📷 *Screenshot: an empty upload slot (dashed box with "Choose file") beside a completed green slot showing the filename, View, and Remove.*

### Slots that take several files (bank statements)

Some slots accept more than one file — bank statements are the main example, where you upload three monthly statements into a single slot per parent. The drop-zone stays visible while a list of the files you've added builds up underneath, each with its own **View** and **Remove**. Add each statement in turn (or select several at once in the file picker). To fix a mistake, remove just the one file — the others stay put. Full detail: [Upload three months of bank statements](walkthroughs/applicants/17-upload-bank-statements-multi-file.md).

### Replacing a file

Uploaded the wrong file, or got a clearer copy? Before you submit, click **Remove** on the slot, then upload the replacement the same way. For a multi-file slot, remove just the file you want to swap. Removing a file deletes it permanently, so have the replacement ready first. After you submit, uploads are locked — if the team needs a new copy they'll ask you directly. Full detail: [Replace a document](walkthroughs/applicants/18-replace-a-document.md).

### File types and sizes

Files must be **PDF, JPEG, or PNG**, up to **20 MB** each. If a file is rejected, the slot turns red with a message — usually "File too large" or "Unsupported file type". The walkthrough explains how to shrink an oversized PDF, convert an iPhone **HEIC** photo to JPEG, or turn a Word document into a PDF. Full detail: [What file types and sizes are accepted](walkthroughs/applicants/19-file-types-and-sizes.md).

### Photographing a paper document with your phone

No scanner needed. On your phone, tap **Choose file** on the slot and pick **Take a photo** (iPhone) or **Camera** (Android). Lay the document on a plain, dark surface in good light, fill the frame, and hold steady — blur is the most common reason a document is unreadable. For multi-page documents, use your phone's built-in scanner (iPhone: Notes app → camera → **Scan Documents**; Android: Google Drive → **+** → **Scan**) to combine pages into one PDF. Full detail: [Upload from your phone's camera](walkthroughs/applicants/20-upload-from-phone-camera.md).

## Reviewing and submitting

When you've worked through the sections, you arrive at the **Review Your Application** page — the last checkpoint before the declaration.

> 📷 *Screenshot: the review page showing the progress bar, the issues panel with "Go fix this" links, and the section cards with status pills.*

### The review page

The page shows a progress bar, then one of two things:

- If anything required is missing, a **red issues panel** headed **N issues to resolve before you can submit**. Each issue has a short description and a **Go fix this** link that takes you straight to the right field in the right section. Fix it, save, and you're brought back here.
- If nothing is missing, a **green banner** reading **Looks complete — review your details below and proceed to the Declaration when ready.**

Below that, each of the nine data-entry sections appears on its own card with a status pill — **Complete** (green), **Needs attention** (red), or **Not started** (grey) — an **Edit** button, and a summary of what you entered. Use **Edit** to change anything. Full detail: [The pre-submission review page](walkthroughs/applicants/21-pre-submission-review-page.md).

### Why Submit might be disabled

The **Proceed to Declaration** button stays greyed out until everything required is in place. The usual reasons are a required field left empty, a required document missing from a slot, or a conditional field that appeared (because you answered "Yes" somewhere) and wasn't filled in. Every reason is listed in the issues panel. If the panel looks empty but the button is still disabled, refresh the page — your most recent save may not have synced yet.

### Signing and submitting

Once the page is clear, click **Proceed to Declaration**, read the ten-point declaration, tick the agreement box, and type your full name in **On behalf of the applicant**. Then click **Submit Application**. Full detail: [Submit your application](walkthroughs/applicants/22-submit-application.md).

### What happens the moment you submit

You're taken to a green confirmation page, **Your application has been submitted**, showing your **application reference** (a unique code — write it down), the submission date, the child's name, and the round. A **What happens next?** list explains the four stages (confirmation email → document review → financial assessment → outcome) and shows the Foundation's target decision date.

Within a few minutes you should receive a **confirmation email** quoting the same reference. If it hasn't arrived after fifteen minutes, check your spam folder; if the inbox is shared, ask the others on it; and if there's still nothing, contact the bursary team to confirm receipt.

From this point your application becomes **read-only** — you can view it but not change it — and your status moves from **Draft** to **Submitted**. Tip: save or screenshot the confirmation page so you always have your reference to hand.

## After you submit

Once submitted, the portal becomes a way to track your application. Sign in any time and click **View Status** to see exactly where things stand.

> 📷 *Screenshot: the Application Status page showing the reference card, the four-step timeline, and the decision-date notice.*

### Checking your status

The status page shows a card with your **reference number**, the child's name, the round, and a coloured **status pill**. Below it is a four-step **timeline** — **Draft → Submitted → Under Review → Outcome Available** — with the date each step was reached. Reached steps are gold; steps still to come are grey and read "Pending". A blue banner tells you when decisions for your round are expected.

Here's what each status means:

| Status | What it means |
| --- | --- |
| **Draft** | You're still filling in the form. Not yet sent. |
| **Submitted** | You've submitted. Waiting for an assessor to pick it up. |
| **Under Review** | An assessor is actively working on your file. |
| **Paused** | The assessor is waiting on something from you — usually a missing document. Check your email for a request. |
| **Completed / Qualifies / Does Not Qualify** | The decision has been made. |

You'll always get an email at the major milestones, so the status page is a convenience rather than something you need to watch. A status that hasn't changed for a while is normal during a busy round. Full detail: [Check the status of your application](walkthroughs/applicants/23-check-application-status.md).

### Responding to a "missing documents" request

Sometimes an assessor needs something more — a clearer bank statement, a missing payslip, or evidence for something you described in Section 9. They'll **email you**, with a subject like *"More information needed for your bursary application"*. The email explains which document is needed and why, gives a link back into the portal, and usually sets a deadline (often 7 working days). While the request is open, your status shows **Paused**.

To respond, click the link, sign in, and upload the requested file(s) using the same upload mechanics as before. Respond as quickly as you can — decisions are batched by round, so a delay can push your outcome to a later batch. If the request is unclear or you can't get the document in time, just reply to the email; the team would rather hear from you.

> Please note: the in-portal page for these requests is still being built. Until it's live, the **email itself** will guide you on exactly what to send and how — so always read the email carefully and follow its instructions.

Full detail: [Respond to a "missing documents" request](walkthroughs/applicants/24-respond-to-missing-documents-request.md).

### Reading your outcome

When the assessment is complete you'll get an email (subject *"Bursary application outcome"*) and the status page will show a new outcome card. There are two outcomes:

- **Qualifies — a green card** reading *"Your application qualifies for a bursary."* The Foundation will then send a separate letter or email with the **percentage of fees** your bursary covers and the **Bursary Contract** for you to sign and return before the school year starts. Bursaries are reviewed every year (see re-assessment, below).
- **Does Not Qualify — a rose-coloured card** reading *"Your application does not qualify at this time."* A letter follows with the headline reason. By policy the Foundation doesn't give a line-by-line breakdown, but if your circumstances change significantly you may be invited to re-apply in a future round.

Decisions are final. If you believe an important piece of information was missed or miscounted, write to the bursary team within **14 days** of receiving the outcome, explaining the specific issue, and they'll tell you whether the application can be looked at again. Save the outcome email and any follow-up letter together — you may need them for the school's fees office or for tax. Full detail: [Read your outcome](walkthroughs/applicants/25-read-your-outcome.md).

## Returning families: re-assessment (year two and beyond)

Bursaries are reviewed every year. At the start of each new academic year, the Foundation invites every active bursary holder to complete a **re-assessment** — a shorter version of the original application.

### Accepting a re-assessment invitation

You'll get an email (subject like *"Bursary re-assessment for [child's name]"*) with a link to the portal. Click it and **sign in with the same email and password** you used last year (reset the password if you've forgotten it). You'll be welcomed back to the new round and asked to confirm the school and your child's name, then taken into the shorter form. Full detail: [Accept a re-assessment invitation](walkthroughs/applicants/26-accept-reassessment-invitation.md).

If you weren't expecting an invitation, or you don't wish to re-apply, contact the bursary team — note that **not responding to a re-assessment usually leads to the bursary lapsing**.

### What's different in a re-assessment

- **Section 2 (Family Identification) is skipped.** The Foundation already has everyone's ID from year one, so no new passports are needed unless your family has changed (if a new dependant joined, contact the team — they may check ID separately).
- **Some sections are pre-filled** from last year — Details of Child, Parent/Guardian Details, Dependent Children, and Dependent Elderly. You'll see a small **"Pre-filled from last year — please confirm"** banner. Check each, update anything that's changed, and confirm. Take care not to blank out a pre-filled field by accident.
- **All the financial sections start blank** — Income, Assets & Liabilities, and Other Information. You re-enter this year's figures fresh and upload new documents (new P60s, the latest council tax bill, and three months of recent bank statements). This is the bulk of the work, so set aside an evening to gather the documents first.

The sidebar shows nine sections instead of ten. You finish the same way as in year one — review page, then declaration and submit. Full detail: [Complete a re-assessment](walkthroughs/applicants/27-complete-reassessment.md).

> Please note: the re-assessment-specific screens (the skipped section and the pre-fill banners) are still being finished. The flow above is the intended experience.

### How the Foundation reasons about year-on-year changes

This is background — there's nothing to do in the portal, but it helps you read your outcome letter.

The Foundation uses **last year's payable fees** (your share of the fees after last year's bursary) as a **benchmark**. If your finances are roughly unchanged, your contribution stays roughly the same and the bursary absorbs any rise in school fees. Where your award changes materially, the outcome letter explains why using short, plain-English **reason codes** — for example *Increase in income*, *Reduction in income*, *New dependant*, *Property sale/purchase*, *Inheritance received*, or *Redundancy/job loss* — and the direction (up or down) each pushed your award.

The practical lesson: if a number looks unusually high or low this year for a one-off reason, **explain it in Section 9**. That lets the assessor apply a one-off reason rather than treating it as a permanent change. Always declare the new figures honestly — a genuine drop in income may well increase your bursary. A large change in either direction usually means the assessor will want a friendly conversation; that's normal, not a problem. Full detail: [What changes year-on-year](walkthroughs/applicants/28-what-changes-year-on-year.md).

## Your data and privacy

Your application contains sensitive personal and financial information, and the Foundation handles it carefully. You only ever see your own data — never another family's, and never the assessors' internal calculations.

Under **UK GDPR** you have the right to ask for your personal data to be deleted. There is no self-service "delete my account" button — because deletion is irreversible, the Foundation handles it manually so it's done correctly.

**What gets deleted:** your profile (name, email, phone, address), all your application data, every uploaded document, your assessment record, and your login account.

**What is kept:** a small amount of **audit-trail** information (with your name and email removed) recording that actions took place, and — if applicable — the bare fact that a bursary was awarded for a given year, with no identifying detail. This is kept for legal and audit reasons.

**The 7-year rule:** if your application is less than seven years old and tied to an active or recently-closed bursary, the Foundation is legally required to retain the records until that window ends. In that case the deletion is **scheduled** for when the period expires, rather than done immediately, and the team confirms this in writing.

To request deletion, email the bursary team, state clearly that you're making a **UK GDPR deletion request (Article 17)**, and include the child's name and your application reference. They'll reply within one calendar month confirming the deletion has been carried out or scheduled. Before you ask, save any outcome letters or bursary contracts you want to keep — once deletion runs, nothing can be recovered. If you might re-apply later, consider asking the team to **close** your account instead of deleting it. Full detail: [Request your data be deleted](walkthroughs/applicants/29-request-data-deletion.md).

## If something goes wrong

Most problems have a quick fix. Here are the common ones.

| Problem | What's happening | What to do |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **"Invitation invalid" when I click the link** | The link has expired or was already used. | Try signing in (you may already have an account), or email the bursary team for a fresh invitation. See [walkthrough 02](walkthroughs/applicants/02-invitation-link-expired.md). |
| **Password rejected as too common / breached** | The system blocks known-leaked passwords. | Choose a longer, unique password — a phrase of three random words works well. |
| **"Passwords do not match"** | The two password boxes differ. | Re-type both carefully. |
| **No invitation email at all** | It may be in spam, or the Foundation has an old address. | Check spam; if nothing, reply to the team using the contact details they gave you. |
| **Forgotten my password** | — | Use **Forgot password?** on the login page. See [walkthrough 03](walkthroughs/applicants/03-reset-forgotten-password.md). |
| **No password-reset email arrives** | For privacy, the system never confirms whether an address is registered. | Wait a couple of minutes, check spam, confirm you used the right address; if still nothing, contact the team. |
| **"Too many attempts"** on password reset | The system limits repeated reset requests. | Wait a few minutes and try again. |
| **Page looks zoomed or doesn't fit on my phone** | Display quirk on a small screen. | Pinch to zoom out, or rotate to landscape. |
| **Camera upload is greyed out** | Your browser doesn't have camera permission. | Grant camera access in your phone's settings, then retry. |
| **File rejected: "File too large — maximum 20 MB"** | The file exceeds the limit. | Compress the PDF or take a smaller photo. See [walkthrough 19](walkthroughs/applicants/19-file-types-and-sizes.md). |
| **File rejected: "Unsupported file type"** | Only PDF, JPG, and PNG are accepted. | Convert it first (e.g. "Save as PDF", or HEIC → JPEG). See [walkthrough 19](walkthroughs/applicants/19-file-types-and-sizes.md). |
| **Upload fails with a red "Try again"** | Often a brief network glitch. | Click **Try again**; check your connection. |
| **Submit button is greyed out** | Something required is still missing. | Read the issues panel and use each **Go fix this** link. If the panel is empty, refresh the page. See [walkthrough 21](walkthroughs/applicants/21-pre-submission-review-page.md). |
| **A section shows amber after I removed a file** | Removing a required document made the section incomplete. | Upload a replacement; the indicator returns to green. |
| **No confirmation email after submitting** | It may be in spam, or sent to a shared inbox. | Wait fifteen minutes, check spam, ask others on the inbox; if still nothing, contact the team to confirm receipt. |
| **My status hasn't changed in a while** | Normal during busy parts of a round. | Wait for the milestone email; contact the team if genuinely concerned. |

If your problem isn't here, the bursary team is happy to help — see [Getting help and contact](#getting-help-and-contact).

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I have to apply — can I sign myself up?**
No. Applications are invitation-only. The Foundation sends you an invitation by email; you can't self-register. If you expected an invitation and haven't had one, contact the bursary team.

**Can I start on my phone and finish on a laptop?**
Yes. Your work saves section by section and follows you between devices. Many people photograph documents on their phone, then finish typing on a laptop.

**Do I have to fill the sections in order?**
No. You can complete the ten sections in any order and jump around using the sidebar. Each section saves on its own.

**Do both parents need their own account?**
No. Only the invited parent or guardian has a login. You complete the whole form on behalf of the household, including both parents' details. There's no second-parent login.

**I'm a sole parent — do I still fill in a second parent's details?**
No. Tick **Are you a sole parent?** in Section 3 and all the Parent 2 blocks disappear throughout the form.

**Who sees my information?**
Only the Foundation's bursary staff who assess your application. You only ever see your own data, and you never see the assessors' internal calculations. Your financial information is used solely for the bursary process.

**What documents do I need?**
The full list is in [Before you begin](#before-you-begin-what-youll-need) and the [Document checklist](#document-checklist). The essentials are the birth certificate, passports (plus right-to-remain papers for non-British citizens), P60s, three months of bank statements, and your council tax bill.

**What file types can I upload, and how big can they be?**
PDF, JPEG, or PNG, up to 20 MB each. A phone photo is fine. See [Accepted file types and size limits](#accepted-file-types-and-size-limits).

**Can I change my answers after I submit?**
No. Once you submit, the application becomes read-only. If the team needs a correction, they'll contact you with a request.

**What if I miss the deadline?**
Contact the bursary team as soon as you can. They can advise on your options and, if appropriate, re-issue an invitation. Don't assume the door is closed — get in touch.

**How long until I hear back?**
The status page and the submission confirmation both show the target decision date for your round. Busy rounds can take a little longer. You'll always get an email when the outcome is ready.

**What does "Paused" mean on my status?**
The assessor is waiting on something from you — usually a missing or unclear document. Check your email for a request and respond as soon as you can.

**My income changed a lot this year — what should I do?**
Declare the real figures and explain the reason in Section 9 (Additional Information). A one-off change can be treated differently from a permanent one, and a genuine drop in income may increase your bursary.

**My application doesn't qualify — can I appeal?**
Decisions are final, but if you think an important fact was missed or miscounted, write to the team within 14 days explaining the specific issue. They'll tell you whether it can be looked at again.

**How do I get my data deleted?**
Email the team stating you're making a UK GDPR deletion request. Note the 7-year retention rule may apply if you have an active or recent bursary. See [Your data and privacy](#your-data-and-privacy).

**I'm a returning family — is the form the same?**
It's shorter. The identity section is skipped, several sections are pre-filled from last year for you to confirm, and the financial sections start blank for the new year. See [Returning families](#returning-families-re-assessment-year-two-and-beyond).

## Appendix

### Glossary

| Term | Plain meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Bursary** | Financial help with school fees, awarded by the Foundation to families who need it. |
| **Scholarship** | An award based on a child's ability or achievement, separate from a bursary (which is based on financial need). |
| **Gross income** | Your income **before** tax and other deductions are taken off. |
| **Net income** | Your income **after** tax — your take-home pay. (The form asks for gross figures.) |
| **Payable fees** | The part of the school fees **you** pay after your bursary is applied. |
| **Benchmark** | Last year's payable fees, used as the starting point when re-assessing your award. |
| **Re-assessment** | The shorter annual review of an existing bursary, done at the start of each academic year. |
| **Reason code** | A short, plain-English label the Foundation uses in an outcome letter to explain why an award changed (e.g. *Increase in income*). |
| **Declaration** | The legal statement at the end of the form confirming your information is true; you sign it by typing your name. |
| **P60** | The end-of-year summary your employer gives you showing your total pay and tax for the year. |
| **SA302 / Self Assessment** | The HMRC tax return summary for people who are self-employed or who file a tax return. |
| **Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR)** | A document showing a non-British citizen has permission to live in the UK permanently. |
| **Right to remain** | Any document (visa, ILR, etc.) showing a non-British family member's permission to live in the UK. |
| **Sole parent** | A single parent or guardian applying on their own; ticking this hides the second-parent sections. |
| **Dependant** | Someone who relies on you financially — a child or an elderly relative. |
| **Audit trail** | A record of when actions happened, kept for legal reasons even after personal details are removed. |
| **UK GDPR** | The UK's data-protection law, which gives you rights over your personal data, including the right to ask for it to be deleted. |
