# 01 — Accept your invitation and set up your account

Backlink: [[README#Getting started]]

You can't sign yourself up for a bursary — the Foundation sends you
an invitation by email. This guide walks you through opening that
link and setting up your account for the first time.

## What you need

- The invitation email from the John Whitgift Foundation (subject
  line will mention "bursary" or "invitation").
- A password you can remember — it must be at least **12
  characters**.

## Steps

1. Open the invitation email and click the link inside it. It will
   take you to a page titled **Create your account**.
2. Your **Email** is shown at the top of the form and cannot be
   changed — it has to match the one the Foundation invited.
3. Check that your **First name** and **Last name** are correct. If
   the Foundation pre-filled them and a spelling is off, you can
   edit them here.
4. In **Choose a password**, type a password of at least 12
   characters. Choose something unique — the system checks your
   password against a list of known-breached passwords and will
   reject anything common.
5. Type the same password again in **Confirm password**.
6. Click **Create account**.
7. You'll be signed in automatically and taken to your bursary
   dashboard, where you'll see a welcome message and a card
   inviting you to set up your application.

## After you sign in

You'll see a card titled **Let's set up your application** asking
you to confirm:

- The **school** your child has been offered a place at (Trinity
  School or Whitgift School), and
- Your **child's full name** as it appears on their birth
  certificate.

Confirm both and click the button to enter the form. From now on
you can sign in any time at the portal login page using your email
address and the password you just set.

## If something goes wrong

- **"Invitation invalid"** — the link is expired or has already
  been used. See [[02-invitation-link-expired]].
- **"Passwords do not match"** — re-type both password boxes
  carefully.
- **Password rejected as too common or breached** — pick a longer,
  more unique password. A short phrase of three random words works
  well.
- **You don't see an invitation email at all** — check your spam
  folder. If still nothing, reply to the Foundation's bursary team
  using the contact details they previously gave you.
