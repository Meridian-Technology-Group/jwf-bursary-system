# 02 — If your invitation link expired or doesn't work

Backlink: [[README#Getting started]]

Invitation links are time-limited. If yours stopped working before
you got round to using it, the Foundation can send you a fresh
one.

## What you'll see

When you click an expired or already-used link, the page will read:

> **Invitation invalid**
>
> *(followed by a short explanation, such as "this invitation has
> expired" or "this invitation has already been accepted")*

There's a **Back to sign in** button at the bottom.

## What to do

1. Don't panic — no data is lost. The invitation simply needs to
   be re-sent.
2. Email the John Whitgift Foundation's bursary team. The contact
   address is in the original invitation email. Quote:
   - The name of the child the invitation was for, and
   - The date the invitation was originally sent (if you remember).
3. The team will issue a new invitation. You'll receive a fresh
   email with a new link — use that one instead.

## Common reasons a link fails

- **Expired.** Invitations have a limited lifetime. Once that
  window passes the link stops working.
- **Already used.** If you (or someone else with access to your
  inbox) already accepted the invitation, the link can only be
  used once. Go to the login page and sign in with the password
  you set the first time — or use [[03-reset-forgotten-password]]
  if you don't remember it.
- **Email forwarded with broken formatting.** Long links can wrap
  awkwardly when forwarded. Open the original email instead of a
  forwarded copy.
- **Wrong email address.** The invitation is tied to one specific
  email address. If the Foundation has an old address for you,
  reply to them and ask for it to be updated.
