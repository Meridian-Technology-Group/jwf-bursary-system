# 12 — Section 7: Parents' Income

Backlink: [[README#Filling in the application]]

This section records each parent's **gross annual income** from
every source. You'll see two columns (Parent 1 and Parent 2) — or
just one if you ticked "sole parent" in section 3.

Have your most recent P60, self-assessment SA302, benefits letters
and any other income statements to hand.

## Income lines

For each parent, the form lists 14 rows. Enter a number in each
(zero if not applicable):

- Salary / wages, state or private pension(s)
- Any supplement(s) and/or bonus
- Any other benefits and commission(s)
- Amount supplied by partner
- Working tax credits
- Gross interest received (on deposits)
- All dividend income (UK or overseas)
- Gross rent(s) received
- All income (bonds)
- Other gross income(s)
- Maintenance or equivalent(s)
- Bursaries / sponsorships
- Other income not included above
- Other income

A running **Total** for each parent appears underneath.

## Required uploads

The system asks for documents depending on what you've entered:

- **P60** — always required for each parent (the most recent
  one).
- **Self-assessment (SA302)** — required if you entered any
  dividend income, gross rents, or bond income above zero.
- **Benefits evidence** — required if working tax credits or
  other benefits are above zero. Upload your award letter.
- **Capital repayments evidence** — required if you ticked the
  "capital repayments" question further down.

Each upload slot accepts PDF, JPG, or PNG, up to 20 MB. See
[[16-upload-a-document]].

## Steps

1. Working down each parent's column, type a number into every
   row. Use the gross (pre-tax) figure.
2. Upload each required document into its slot. The slot label
   tells you exactly which document goes there (e.g. "P60 — Parent
   1").
3. Click **Save and Continue**.

## Tips

- All figures are **annual gross** — multiply monthly amounts by
  12 first.
- If a parent has no income, put zero in every row and explain in
  section 9 (additional information) if that's unusual.
- If you're not sure which row a payment fits — check the
  guidance text under the row, or contact the bursary team rather
  than guessing.
