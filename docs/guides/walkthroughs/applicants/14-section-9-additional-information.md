# 14 — Section 9: Additional Information

Backlink: [[README#Filling in the application]]

This is your chance to flag any special circumstances and to add
anything the Foundation should know that didn't fit elsewhere.

## Circumstances checklist

A list of six common situations. For each, answer Yes or No:

- **Divorced (if applicable)**
- **Separated (if applicable)**
- **Sick / unable to work**
- **Paying rent (current statement or lease)**
- **Been made redundant or lost employment**
- **Receiving benefits**

When you tick **Yes** on any row, an upload prompt appears for the
relevant supporting document (decree absolute, sick note, lease,
redundancy letter, benefits award letter, etc.).

## Free-text narrative

Below the checklist is a large text box labelled **Additional
information**. Use this for anything else:

- One-off events (an inheritance, a death in the family, a major
  business loss).
- Recent changes that won't show up in the historical figures yet.
- Anything that explains an unusual number in sections 7 or 8.

The limit is **3,000 characters** (about 500 words). A character
counter appears under the box.

## Steps

1. Tick **Yes** or **No** on each circumstance row.
2. For each **Yes**, upload the supporting document into the slot
   that appears.
3. Type any additional context in the narrative box.
4. Click **Save and Continue**.

## Tips

- It's fine to leave the narrative blank. If your situation is
  straightforward, less is more.
- Keep the tone factual: dates, amounts, who, what. Assessors
  appreciate brevity.
