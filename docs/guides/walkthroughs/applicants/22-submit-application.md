# 22 — Submit your application

Backlink: [[README#Before you submit]]

You've reached the end of the form. This guide covers the final
click and what happens next.

## Steps

1. From the review page (see [[21-pre-submission-review-page]]),
   click **Proceed to Declaration**.
2. Read the ten-point declaration carefully. See
   [[15-section-10-declaration]] for what each point means.
3. Tick the box reading **By clicking this box, I/we agree to the
   terms and conditions of the declaration set out above…**.
4. Type your full name in **On behalf of the applicant**.
5. Click **Submit Application** in the bottom bar.

## What happens immediately

You're taken to a confirmation page titled **Your application has
been submitted** (green banner) showing:

- Your **application reference** (a unique code — write it down).
- The **submission date**.
- The child's name.
- The assessment round.

Underneath, a **What happens next?** list explains the four-step
process (confirmation email → document review → financial
assessment → outcome notification) and shows the Foundation's
target decision date for your round.

## Confirmation email

Within a few minutes you should receive an email titled something
like *"Your John Whitgift Foundation bursary application has been
received"*. It will quote the same reference number.

If you don't see it after fifteen minutes:

- Check your spam folder.
- The email is sent to the address you used to register. If that
  inbox is shared, ask the other people on it.
- If still nothing, contact the bursary team — they can confirm
  receipt directly.

## After submission

- The form becomes **read-only**. You can still view it but not
  change anything.
- Your status moves from **Draft** to **Submitted** — see
  [[23-check-application-status]].
- If the team later needs more from you, you'll be contacted —
  see [[24-respond-to-missing-documents-request]].

## Tips

- Save or screenshot the confirmation page so you have your
  reference to hand.
- The Foundation aims to communicate the outcome by the decision
  date shown on the page, but it can take longer in busy rounds.
  Be patient — they will get to you.
