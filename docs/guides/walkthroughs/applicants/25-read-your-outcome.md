# 25 — Read your outcome

Backlink: [[README#After submission]]

When the assessment is complete, you'll receive an email and the
status page will show the result.

## Where to find it

- An **email** is sent to your registered address. Subject line:
  *"Bursary application outcome — *(reference)*"*.
- The **status page** (see [[23-check-application-status]]) shows
  a new outcome card and the timeline's fourth step turns gold.

## The two outcomes

### Qualifies — bursary awarded

A **green** card on the status page reads:

> **Your application qualifies for a bursary.**
>
> *Congratulations. The John Whitgift Foundation will be in touch
> with further details about your bursary award.*

What happens next:

- The Foundation sends a separate letter or email with the
  **percentage of fees** your bursary covers and the **Bursary
  Contract** for you to sign.
- You'll need to sign and return the contract before the start of
  the school year.
- The bursary is reviewed each year — see [[26-accept-reassessment-invitation]].

### Does Not Qualify

A **rose-coloured** card reads:

> **Your application does not qualify at this time.**
>
> *We regret to inform you that your application has not met the
> criteria for a bursary at this time. You will receive a letter
> with further information.*

What happens next:

- The Foundation sends a letter or email with the reasoning. By
  policy, they don't provide a line-by-line breakdown, but they
  will explain the headline reason.
- If your circumstances change significantly, you may be invited
  to re-apply in a future round.

## What if I disagree?

Decisions are final, but if you believe an important piece of
information was missed or miscounted, write to the bursary team
within 14 days of receiving the outcome explaining the specific
issue. They will tell you whether the application can be looked
at again.

## Tips

- Save the outcome email and the follow-up letter together — you
  may need them later for the school's fees office or for tax
  purposes.
- If your award is conditional (e.g. on a sibling continuing at
  the school), make sure you understand the condition before
  signing the Bursary Contract.
