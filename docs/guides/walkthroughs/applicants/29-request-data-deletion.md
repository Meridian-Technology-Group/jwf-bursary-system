# 29 — Request your data be deleted

Backlink: [[README#Account and privacy]]

Under UK GDPR you have the right to ask for your personal data to
be deleted. The Foundation can process this request on your
behalf.

## What can be deleted

When the Foundation acts on a deletion request:

- Your **profile** — name, email, phone, address.
- Your **application data** — every section's content.
- **All uploaded documents** — birth certificate, passports,
  bank statements, P60s, council tax bill, and any others.
- Your **assessment record and recommendation** (if any).
- Your **login account**.

## What is retained

For legal and audit reasons, a small amount of data is kept:

- **Audit-trail entries** — when each action happened (but with
  your name and email removed). These prove the deletion took
  place and demonstrate that the Foundation followed its own
  process.
- **The fact a bursary was awarded** for the relevant academic
  year (if applicable), with no personally identifying detail.

## The 7-year retention rule

If your application is **less than 7 years old** and is tied to
an **active or recently-closed bursary**, the Foundation is
legally required to retain the records until the 7-year window
expires. In that case the deletion will be **scheduled** for the
date the retention period ends rather than executed immediately.
The bursary team will confirm in writing.

## How to request deletion

There's no self-service "delete my account" button in the portal.
Deletion is irreversible, so the Foundation handles it manually:

1. Email the bursary team. Use the contact details from your
   invitation email or outcome letter.
2. State clearly that you are making a **UK GDPR deletion
   request** under Article 17.
3. Include the **child's name** and your **application
   reference** (if you have it).
4. Optionally, ask whether the 7-year retention window applies
   to your record.
5. The team will reply within one calendar month confirming
   either that deletion has been carried out or that it has been
   scheduled (with the scheduled date) along with the reason.

## Before you ask

Once deletion has run, you cannot recover anything — the
documents are deleted from storage, and the answers in the form
are gone. If you might want to **re-apply in future**, consider
asking the Foundation to **close** your account instead — you'll
still be able to re-invite yourself later without starting fully
from scratch.

## Tips

- Save any outcome letters or bursary contracts you want to keep
  **before** asking for deletion — those are your records, and
  you may need them for the school's fees office or for tax.
- If you only want to update an email address or change your
  password, you don't need a deletion request — just sign in and
  use [[03-reset-forgotten-password]] or contact the bursary team
  to update your address.
