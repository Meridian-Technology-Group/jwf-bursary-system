# JWF Buyer Meeting — Talking Points

> 60 min · Video · CEO, Head of Impact, Head of Fees (champion). CFO absent.
> Goal: leave with a Friday decision deadline agreed.

---

## Section 1 — Solution Demo (35 min)

**Open (2 min)** — set the frame before clicking anything.
- "What you're about to see isn't a prototype — it's the working system. ~80% of the deliverables are built and running."
- "I'll walk it from two angles: a parent applying, then the assessor working through that application."
- Acknowledge Head of Fees has seen most of this; tell CEO + Head of Impact this is their first proper look.

**Applicant Portal (8 min)** — the parent's experience.
- Land on dashboard → progress indicator across 10 sections.
- Walk Section 1 (Child Details) — entry year dropdown, school, DOB.
- Jump to a financial section — show conditional logic (sole parent, employment status).
- Document upload slot — show named slots, format/size constraints.
- Validation summary screen — list of missing fields, click-through.
- Mobile responsive note: "Many parents complete this on a phone."

**Admin Queue (3 min)** — the assessor's day starts here.
- Show queue with names hidden by default — emphasise GDPR-by-design.
- Toggle "Show Names" — note this is audit-logged.
- Filter by status, school, round. Click into an application.

**Assessment Form (12 min) — the headline feature.** Slow down here.
- Tab 1: Applicant Data — read-only view of what the family submitted.
- Tab 2: Assessment — open the split-screen.
- Show docs in left panel (P60 / bank statement) while entering NET figures in right panel.
- Auto-population: pick family type → notional rent, food, utilities populate. Pick school → fees populate.
- Watch the four-stage calc update live: Income → Assets → Living Costs → Bursary.
- Manual adjustment field with reason — "the assessor's judgment is preserved."
- Reference number in header, anonymised "Parent 1 / Parent 2" labels — cover NM-02.

**Recommendation + Export (4 min)**
- Generate a recommendation with reason codes.
- Export XLSX → open in Excel/Numbers, show the school-facing structure.
- Generate the PDF summary.

**Reports + Dashboard (3 min)**
- Live tiles, activity feed.
- One or two charts from the Reports page (bursary awards by school, reason code frequency).

**GDPR Controls (2 min)** — close strong on trust.
- Audit trail page (filter by entity).
- 2-step deletion dialog (don't actually delete).
- "Every name reveal, every edit, every export — all logged."

**Wrap (1 min)** — "Questions before I move into the pitch?"

## Section 2 — The Pitch (10 min)

Lead with framing, weave the USPs — don't read them off as a list.

**Opening frame (1 min)**
- "You were initially worried building your own was too novel. I want to address that head-on — because the landscape has changed."
- "Three years ago, I would have told you to buy SaaS. Today, with modern AI tooling, bespoke is faster and cheaper than fighting an off-the-shelf product into shape."

**Why me, why Meridian (2 min)** — credentials, briefly.
- 25 years building software. Ford, Cisco, Amazon (built into banks), Silicon Valley analytics — director-level.
- 5 years as a serial CTO.
- CISSP certified. Cybersecurity author.
- Meridian has been running since 2011 under various names — this is not a side project or a startup.
- "If I can build trading systems for banks, I can build a bursary platform for two schools."

**Bespoke without the bespoke price tag (2 min)** — USP #4.
- "SaaS is dead. The economics flipped."
- AI-assisted pipelines mean I deliver work in a fraction of the time it took 3 years ago.
- That savings goes to you — the £5,000 build figure reflects modern productivity, not a discount on shoddy work.
- Cheaper alternatives are off-the-shelf compromises; the more expensive bespoke quote was building the same thing the old way.

**Total fit to your model (1.5 min)** — USP #2.
- "Your assessment model is the IP. The system was designed around it, not adapted to it."
- Four-stage calculation, two-layer data model, sibling absorption, benchmark logic — all native, not workarounds.
- "Symplectic Grant Tracker was built for research grants. This was built for bursaries. That distinction matters every day."

**Reliability (1.5 min)** — USP #3.
- "We're 9 weeks from go-live and the system is largely built. That's not optimism — it's where we are."
- Concrete gates (G1–G4) on the slide deck — show pages 3 and the timeline visual.
- Hypercare window post go-live — daily check-ins for two weeks.

**Open, not locked-in (1 min)** — USP #5.
- "The Foundation owns the source code. No vendor lock-in."
- Standard, mainstream stack — Next.js, Postgres, Supabase. Any competent developer can pick it up if you ever needed to leave Meridian.
- Upgrades, integrations, future modules — all possible without a re-platform.

**Security & GDPR (1 min)** — USP #6, your specialty.
- "I wrote a book on cybersecurity. This isn't a checkbox for me."
- GDPR controls baked into the data model from day one — not retrofitted.
- Independent GDPR review at G3 before go-live.

**Close the pitch (~30 sec)** — "These are the reasons your decision is more straightforward than it might look. Hand back to you."

## Section 3 — Q&A (10 min)

Listen first. Don't pre-empt. Use this section to learn what they're worried about.

**Posture**
- Pen and paper handy — write down every question, even ones you answer immediately.
- If a question is one for the contract phase, say so: "Good question — let's hold that for the business section."
- If you don't know, say so. Never bullshit a CEO or a CISSP-aware audience.

**Questions to invite explicitly if they don't surface**
- "Anything that worries you about going live in June?"
- "Anything you saw in the demo that doesn't match how you actually work?"
- "Anything in your governance model — trustees, board approval — that affects how this gets signed?"

---

## Section 4 — Talking Business (5 min)

Bring up the deck on slide 4 (Commercial) and slide 5 (Licence build-up). Don't re-pitch — anchor on numbers.

**The numbers (1 min)**
- £5,000 build · 100% on contract signature
- £7,000 / year licence · invoiced on go-live · annually thereafter
- Migration: separate, discovery-led
- "These numbers are not negotiating positions — they're already priced fair. No padding to discount away."

**Why now (1 min)**
- "Grant Tracker sunsets 31 December 2026. We have runway, but only if we move."
- "End of June go-live gives the assessor a full quarter on the new system before the next round opens."
- "Every week of decision delay is a week off that buffer."

**The ask (1 min)**
- "I'd like a decision by **Friday next week**. That keeps us on the timeline."
- "If you need longer, tell me what you'd need to see between now and then to be comfortable."
- Pause. Don't fill the silence.

**Migration discovery (1 min)**
- "Discovery is the next concrete step regardless of contract — getting access to Grant Tracker takes time."
- "Want me to start that process this week so it's not gating us in June?"

**Close (1 min)**
- Confirm follow-ups: contract draft, NDA if needed, names of trustees who need to sign-off.
- Restate the Friday ask. Get them to acknowledge it verbally.
- "Anything else you want me to send across before then?"

## Appendix — Anticipated Questions & Objections

**"Why are you so much cheaper than the other bespoke vendor?"**
- Modern AI-assisted engineering. Same outcome, fraction of the labour hours. Their quote reflects how this used to be priced; mine reflects how it's priced today.

**"What happens if you get hit by a bus / take on bigger clients / disappear?"**
- Source code is yours. Stack is mainstream. Any competent dev shop can take over.
- Meridian has a subcontractor bench I scale on demand — Freshdesk + outsourced engineering team means coverage doesn't depend on me being at my desk.
- Happy to add an escrow clause to the contract if it gives comfort.

**"How do we know the calculations match our model?"**
- G2 (UAT) is built around exactly this — at least 10 historical anonymised cases run through the new engine, verified against the existing spreadsheet. We don't go live until parity is proven.

**"What about data residency? Where does our data live?"**
- UK or EU region (Supabase / Vercel) — both GDPR-compliant for UK personal data.
- Encrypted at rest, encrypted in transit, daily backups, point-in-time recovery.

**"What if requirements change after go-live?"**
- The £6,000/year maintenance covers minor enhancements as they come up. New major features quoted separately and transparently.
- "You won't get nickel-and-dimed for a column rename."

**"Can we run the system ourselves?"**
- Technically yes — code is yours. In practice, the licence covers the operational headache (hosting, monitoring, patching) at-cost. Most clients don't want to staff for it.

**"Is AI-generated code safe / production-ready?"**
- Every line is reviewed and tested. AI is a productivity tool, not a code dump. The system has been built with normal engineering rigour — types, tests, code review.
- I'd rather show you the codebase than argue the point.

**"What's your contingency if June slips?"**
- Hard deadline is December 2026 (Grant Tracker sunset), not June. June is the target so the assessor has a full quarter before the next round.
- If we slip 2–4 weeks, no commercial impact — the deal stands.

**"What about training?"**
- Hypercare period covers it. The Head of Fees has already used most of the system.
- "Training" for software this small is mostly "use it and ask Freshdesk when stuck."

**"What if a trustee or the CFO has objections after this meeting?"**
- "Send them my way directly — happy to do a 30-min call to address concerns. I'd rather answer the question than have them assume an answer."

## One-page cheat sheet (read just before the call)

- **Tone:** confident, not arrogant. The work speaks; let it.
- **Listen for:** what the CEO and Head of Impact actually care about — they haven't seen this before. Their concerns will be different from Head of Fees.
- **Don't volunteer:** payment phasing concessions, scope reductions, discount on £5K, free maintenance hours.
- **Do volunteer:** Friday deadline. Migration discovery start. Direct line to address trustee concerns.
- **Numbers:** £5,000 + £7,000/yr · go-live end June · decision by Friday.
- **Power phrases:**
  - "SaaS is dead — bespoke is now faster and cheaper."
  - "Your assessment model is the IP. The system was built around it, not adapted to it."
  - "These numbers aren't negotiating positions."
  - "Every week of delay is a week off the buffer."
