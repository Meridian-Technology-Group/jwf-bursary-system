# 07 — Section 2: Family Identification

Backlink: [[README#Filling in the application]]

This section asks for ID documents (passport / right-to-remain) for
**every family member** — that includes the child, both parents (or
the sole parent), any other dependent children, and any dependent
elderly relatives you'll list in later sections.

**Re-assessments skip this section** — you only complete it in
year one.

## Before you start

Gather an ID document for each person in the household:

- British citizens — a passport (or the page of your child's
  passport showing nationality).
- Non-British citizens — passport plus right-to-remain / visa /
  ILR (Indefinite Leave to Remain) document.

PDF or photo, each up to 20 MB.

## Steps

1. At the top of the page you'll see a blue **Note** banner
   reminding you to include all dependent children and dependent
   elderly family members.
2. Click **Add family member**. A small dialog appears asking for
   the person's full name. Type it and confirm.
3. The person appears as a card on the page. Click the card to
   expand it and:
   - Confirm whether they are a **British citizen** (Yes / No).
   - Upload their passport (and, if not British, the right-to-
     remain document) using the upload slots inside the card.
4. Click **Add family member** again for each additional person.
   Repeat until everyone is covered.
5. To remove someone added by mistake, use the trash-can icon on
   their card.
6. Click **Save and Continue** at the bottom.

## Tips

- The order doesn't matter — add people in whatever order is
  easiest. Assessors cross-check names against sections 1, 3, 4,
  and 5.
- You can return later to add a document if you don't have it on
  hand right now. The section will show **Needs attention** in the
  sidebar until every family member has a complete record.
