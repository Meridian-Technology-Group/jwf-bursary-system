# 20 — Upload from your phone's camera

Backlink: [[README#Uploading documents]]

Got the paper original of a document on your kitchen table? You
can photograph it and upload it straight from your phone — no
scanner needed.

## Steps

1. Open the portal on your phone (see [[04-log-in-on-your-phone]]).
2. Navigate to the section holding the slot you need to fill.
3. Tap **Choose file** on the slot.
4. Your phone shows the standard file-picker. Pick **Take a
   photo** (iOS) or **Camera** (Android).
5. Photograph the document. Tip: place it on a plain dark surface
   under good light, with the whole page in frame.
6. Confirm the photo (tap **Use photo** or the tick icon).
7. The photo uploads automatically. The slot turns green when
   it's done.

## For multi-page documents

If a document runs to several pages (e.g. a court order):

- Snap each page as a separate JPG and upload them into a
  multi-file slot if available.
- Or use your phone's built-in document scanner to combine pages
  into one PDF:
  - **iPhone:** open the Notes app, tap the camera icon, choose
    **Scan Documents**, then share the resulting PDF to the
    portal.
  - **Android:** Google Drive → **+** → **Scan**. Saves as a PDF
    you can then upload.

## Quality tips

- **Crop tight.** No tablecloth, no fingers in the photo.
- **Avoid shadows.** Move the lamp, or use natural daylight.
- **Hold steady.** Blur is the most common reason a document
  becomes unreadable for the assessor.
- **Check it's legible.** Tap the photo before uploading — if you
  can't read the smallest text, neither can the assessor.
