# 24 — Respond to a "missing documents" request

Backlink: [[README#After submission]]

Sometimes an assessor needs something more from you — a clearer
copy of a bank statement, a missing payslip, an SA302, or evidence
for a circumstance you described in section 9. They'll email you
asking for it.

> Note: the in-portal flow for missing-document requests is in
> active development. The behaviour below is the intended
> experience; until the in-portal request page lands, the email
> will guide you through what to send and how.

## What the email looks like

Subject line: typically *"More information needed for your
bursary application — *(reference)*"*.

The body explains:

- Which document(s) the assessor needs.
- Why (e.g. "the bank statement uploaded covered Jan only —
  please add February and March").
- A link back into the portal where you can upload it.
- A deadline (usually 7 working days) by which to respond.

While the request is open, your application status on the portal
changes from **Under Review** to **Paused** — see
[[23-check-application-status]].

## Steps to respond

1. Click the link in the email. You'll be asked to sign in (or
   you may already be signed in).
2. You'll land on a page listing the items the assessor needs.
   Each item is shown with the original slot it relates to (e.g.
   "Bank Statement — Parent 1").
3. Click the upload area for each item and add the requested file.
   Mechanics are the same as during application — see
   [[16-upload-a-document]].
4. When all the requested items are filled in, click **Send to
   assessor** (or similar). A confirmation page appears.

## What happens next

- The assessor receives an email saying you've responded.
- Your status changes back from **Paused** to **Under Review**.
- The assessor picks the file back up. There is no extra wait on
  your side until the outcome is ready.

## Tips

- Respond as quickly as you can — decisions are batched by round,
  and a delayed response can push your outcome to a later batch.
- If the request is unclear or you can't get hold of the document
  in time, reply to the email directly. The bursary team would
  rather hear from you than chase silently.
