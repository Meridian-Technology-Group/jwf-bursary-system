# 26 — Accept a re-assessment invitation

Backlink: [[README#Year two and beyond — re-assessment]]

Bursaries are reviewed every year. At the start of each new
academic round, the Foundation invites every active bursary
holder to complete a re-assessment. This guide walks through
accepting that invitation.

## What the email looks like

Subject line: usually *"Bursary re-assessment for *(child's
name)* — *(academic year)*"*.

The body explains that your bursary is up for annual review,
quotes the new round (e.g. "2027/28"), and includes a link to the
portal.

## Steps

1. Click the link in the email. You'll be asked to **sign in**
   using the same email and password you used last year.
2. After signing in you'll land on the portal dashboard. A card
   appears welcoming you back to the new round and asking you to
   confirm:
   - The **school** the child currently attends.
   - The child's **full name** (pre-filled from last year).
3. Confirm both and click the button to begin.
4. You're taken into the re-assessment form, which is shorter
   than the original application — see [[27-complete-reassessment]].

## How re-assessment differs from a first-year application

- **Section 2 (Family Identification) is skipped.** No new
  passports / ID needed unless your family composition has
  changed.
- **Your address, child details, and family member names are
  pre-filled** from last year. Check them, edit anything that's
  changed.
- **All financial sections (income, assets, liabilities) start
  blank.** You re-enter the new year's figures fresh and upload
  new documents (P60, bank statements, council tax bill).

## If something goes wrong

- **The sign-in fails.** Use [[03-reset-forgotten-password]].
- **You weren't expecting an invitation.** Re-assessment is
  triggered when you have an active bursary at the end of the
  previous round. If you weren't expecting one, contact the
  bursary team to check there hasn't been a mix-up.
- **You don't want to re-apply.** Reply to the email and let the
  bursary team know — they'll close out the file. Note that not
  responding to a re-assessment usually leads to the bursary
  lapsing.
