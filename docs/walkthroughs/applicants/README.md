# Applicant Walkthroughs

You are a parent or guardian applying for a bursary on behalf of your
child at Trinity School or Whitgift School. The Foundation invites
you to apply — you cannot sign yourself up. Once you've accepted
your invitation, you complete the application form over several
sessions (saving as you go), upload the supporting documents the
Foundation needs to verify your family's circumstances, and submit.
After submission you can check the status of your application and,
in subsequent years, complete a shorter re-assessment.

These guides are written for **you**, not the Foundation's staff. The
language stays plain, every step has a screenshot, and every guide
ends with what to do if something doesn't behave as expected.

If you get stuck at any point and can't find the answer here, the
Foundation's bursary team is happy to help — see the contact details
in your invitation email.

## Workflows

Each item below will become its own guide (`<slug>.md`) in this
folder.

### Getting started

- **Accept your invitation and set up your account** — open the link
  in the invitation email, confirm your email address is correct,
  set a password, and log in for the first time.
- **What to do if your invitation link expired or doesn't work** —
  expired links can be re-sent by the Foundation. This guide covers
  what error message to expect and who to email.
- **Reset a forgotten password** — request a reset link by email and
  set a new password.
- **Log in on your phone** — the portal works on iOS and Android. A
  short tour of what each tab looks like on a small screen.

### Filling in the application

The form has **ten sections**. You can complete them in any order
and save progress as you go.

- **Tour of the dashboard** — what each part of the home screen
  means: progress indicator, deadline, "Continue Application"
  button, application status, deadline countdown.
- **Section 1 — Details of Child** — school, year-group, child
  name, date of birth, place of birth, current school, upload the
  birth certificate.
- **Section 2 — Family Identification** — passports and right-to-
  remain documents for each family member. (This section is **not**
  shown for re-assessments — you only complete it in year one.)
- **Section 3 — Parent / Guardian Details** — contact details and
  employment status for each parent. Sole-parent? Tick the box and
  the second parent's section disappears.
- **Section 4 — Dependent Children** — a table; add a row per
  dependent child, including age and whether they are also being
  named on this application.
- **Section 5 — Dependent Elderly** — separate tables for elderly
  relatives at home and in care, if any.
- **Section 6 — Other Information** — court orders / maintenance
  payments, insurance policies, outstanding school fees from prior
  years. Only the items that apply to your family.
- **Section 7 — Parents' Income** — the gross income table.
  Different rows appear depending on each parent's employment status.
- **Section 8 — Assets & Liabilities** — property ownership, savings,
  shares, mortgages, other debts. Also where you upload three months
  of bank statements.
- **Section 9 — Additional Information** — special circumstances
  (recent divorce, redundancy, illness, etc.) and a free-text
  narrative if there's anything else the Foundation should know.
- **Section 10 — Declaration** — read the legal declaration, tick
  the box, type your name, and submit.

### Uploading documents

- **Upload a document** — choose a file from your computer or phone,
  see the upload progress, confirm it appears in the named slot.
- **Upload three months of bank statements (or any multi-file slot)**
  — add each statement as a separate file in the same slot. You can
  remove an individual file if you uploaded the wrong one.
- **Replace a document** — upload a corrected version into the same
  slot; the previous version is replaced. (Works only before
  submission. After submission the application is read-only.)
- **What file types and sizes are accepted** — PDF, JPEG, PNG; up to
  20 MB per file. What to do if your file is rejected.
- **Upload from your phone's camera** — take a photo of a paper
  document and upload it directly.

### Before you submit

- **The pre-submission review page** — what every item on the review
  page means, how to fix incomplete sections, why the Submit button
  may still be disabled.
- **Submit your application** — tick the legal declaration, click
  Submit, confirm the submission, and check for the confirmation
  email.

### After submission

- **Check the status of your application** — what each status label
  means (Submitted, Under Review, Outcome Available), where to look
  on your dashboard.
- **Respond to a "missing documents" request** — what the email
  looks like, where to upload the requested documents, what happens
  next.
- **Read your outcome** — qualifying outcomes, non-qualifying
  outcomes, what each means, what happens next, who to contact with
  questions.

### Year two and beyond — re-assessment

- **Accept a re-assessment invitation** — at the start of each new
  academic year, the Foundation invites you to re-assess. The
  invitation looks similar but the form has fewer sections.
- **Complete a re-assessment** — your address, child details, and
  family member names are pre-filled from last year. The Family
  Identification section is skipped entirely. All financial sections
  (income, assets, liabilities) start blank — you re-enter them
  fresh for the new year and upload new supporting documents.
- **What changes year-on-year** — the Foundation uses last year's
  payable fees as a benchmark and explains the reasons for any
  change with reason codes in the recommendation.

### Account and privacy

- **Request your data be deleted** — under UK GDPR you have the
  right to have your data deleted. The Foundation can do this on
  your behalf; this guide explains what data is removed, what is
  retained for audit, and how to make the request.

### Cross-link

Once you've submitted, the Foundation's bursary team takes over.
You will receive emails at the key milestones. If you'd like to know
more about how your application is reviewed and assessed, the
Foundation publishes a separate "How we assess bursaries" document
— ask the bursary team if you'd like a copy.
